About Me

I'm a first-year B.Tech student in Electronics and Biomedical Engineering with a passionate interest in Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI), EMG/EEG signal processing, and AI-driven healthcare solutions. Inspired by groundbreaking work at Neuralink and Google DeepMind, I'm dedicated to contributing to cutting-edge research that bridges neuroscience, engineering, and artificial intelligence.

My academic journey is focused on developing innovative solutions that can translate neural activities into meaningful applications, particularly for assistive technologies. I aim to publish research papers in this interdisciplinary field and ultimately pursue a career as a research scientist working at the frontier of human-computer interaction.

Research Interests

  • Brain-Computer Interfaces: Exploring EEG and EMG signal processing for assistive technologies and rehabilitation. I'm particularly interested in non-invasive BCI applications that can improve quality of life for individuals with mobility impairments.
  • AI in Healthcare: Investigating applications of generative AI models (like Google's Imagen) for medical diagnostics and treatment planning. My focus is on developing systems that can assist healthcare professionals with more accurate and efficient diagnoses.
  • Machine Learning: Studying deep learning architectures, natural language processing, and reinforcement learning with applications in biomedical signal processing. I'm keen on exploring how these technologies can be optimized for real-time neural signal interpretation.
